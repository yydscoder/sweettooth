From the Tea Gardens of Uji

Uji, a small city just south of Kyoto, has been Japan's most revered matcha-growing region since the 13th century. The combination of misty river valleys, mineral-rich soil, and centuries-old cultivation techniques produces tencha leaves of extraordinary quality. In the final weeks before harvest, the plants are shaded from direct sunlight — a practice that increases chlorophyll and L-theanine, creating matcha's signature color, umami depth, and calming character.

We source our matcha directly from a family-run tea farm that has been cultivating in Uji for four generations. The ceremonial-grade powder we receive is stone-ground in small batches to preserve its volatile aromatics. We add it to our gelato base without delay, so none of that freshness is lost.
